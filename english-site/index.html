Definition

Intercognition refers to the process and analytical level specific to the continuous interaction between two heterogeneous cognitive systems, within which cognitive effects emerge that are not reducible to the properties, capacities, or performances of either system considered in isolation.

Intercognition is distinct from the intelligence of one system or the other, and from their mere functional coupling.

It defines a specific regime of interaction in which the resulting cognitive dynamics cannot be adequately described by individual, collective, distributed, or metacognitive frameworks alone.
